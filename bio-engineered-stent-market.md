<p><ul><li>Metal-based Bio-engineered Stent</li><li>Polymer-based Bio-engineered Stent</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bio-engineered stent market comprises two primary types: metal-based and polymer-based stents. Metal-based bio-engineered stents, often made from materials like stainless steel or cobalt-chromium alloys, provide durability and optimal support in vascular procedures. In contrast, polymer-based stents are typically designed for bioresorbability, gradually dissolving in the body and reducing long-term risks associated with permanent implants. Both types aim to enhance patient outcomes by improving blood flow while minimizing complications post-intervention, catering to varied clinical needs in cardiovascular treatments.</p></p>
