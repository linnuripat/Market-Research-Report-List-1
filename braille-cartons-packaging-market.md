<p><p>The Braille cartons packaging market primarily utilizes two key printing techniques: embossing and screen printing. Embossing involves raising Braille dots on the carton material, ensuring tactile readability for visually impaired consumers. This method offers durability and resistance to wear. In contrast, screen printing applies ink directly onto the surface, allowing for more colorful and detailed designs while incorporating Braille text. Both methods aim to enhance accessibility and inclusivity in product packaging, catering to diverse consumer needs and preferences.</p></p>
