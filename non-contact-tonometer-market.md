<p><p>A Non-contact Tonometer is an ophthalmic device used to measure intraocular pressure (IOP) without direct contact with the eye. It employs a puff of air to flatten the cornea, allowing for a quick and effective assessment of eye pressure, which is essential for diagnosing conditions like glaucoma. The appeal of non-contact tonometry lies in its patient comfort and ease of use, facilitating routine examinations in clinical settings.</p><p>The Non-contact Tonometer Market is set to experience significant growth, with a projected CAGR of 5.4% during the forecast period.
