<p><p>Breast lesion localization techniques are crucial for guiding surgical interventions in breast cancer. Wire localization involves placing a thin wire into the breast tissue to pinpoint the exact location of a lesion before surgery. Radioisotope localization uses a radioactive substance to identify and guide the surgeon to the tumor site, often employed in conjunction with lymph node detection. Magnetic localization utilizes magnetic markers placed in or near a lesion, allowing precise navigation with magnetic sensors, enhancing surgical accuracy and reducing tissue disruption.</p></p>
