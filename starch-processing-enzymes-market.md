<p><p>The starch processing enzymes market comprises several key types, including alpha-amylase, beta-amylase, amyloglucosidase, and others. Alpha-amylase acts to break down starch into simpler sugars during the initial processing stages, enhancing efficiency. Beta-amylase further hydrolyzes these sugars, producing maltose. Amyloglucosidase completely converts starch into glucose, which is essential for various applications, including fermentation. Other market enzymes may include glucoamylase and pullulanase, each serving specialized functions in starch conversion, improving yields and optimizing production processes across industries.</p></p>
